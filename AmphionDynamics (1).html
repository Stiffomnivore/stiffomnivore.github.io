Rotational Motion

Angular Position (θ)

  • Refers to the orientation of a rotating object in free space.
  • Applicable to circles and rotational motion.
  • Represents the direction of the displacement vector from the origin to the location of the revolving object.
  • Unit is in radians (rad), equivalent to fractional values of π.
  • To convert values from degrees to radians, use the appropriate conversion factor.

Radians to Degrees Conversion

To convert radians to degrees, use the formula:
degrees = radians * (180 / π)
For example, if you have an angle of π/2 radians:
degrees = (π/2) * (180 / π) ≈ 90°
or
This can be represented in π = 180 degrees.
Step by step:

Step 1: Write the problem π/2.
Step 2: Replace π with 180 degrees: π/2 -> 180/2 = 90 Degrees

Do not forget to write the degree sign

Rotation Vs Revolution

Rotation:

  • Spinning movement without changing position.
  • Example: Top spinning or Earth's day and night.
Revolution:
  • Movement around an axis.
  • Example: Object moving around a point or axis.

Formula:
Δx = Δθ × R
Where Δx is the arc displacement (SI unit), Δθ is the angular displacement (SI unit), and R is the radius (SI unit).
Problem:
An object rotates counterclockwise on a circular path with a radius of 2 meters. The initial angular displacement is 30 degrees, and it rotates to a final angular displacement of 150 degrees. Calculate the arc displacement.

Given:
  • Initial Angular Displacement (Δθinitial): 30 degrees
  • Final Angular Displacement (Δθfinal): 150 degrees
  • Radius (R): 2 meters

Solution:
Δx = (Δθfinal - Δθinitial) × R
Δx = (150° - 30°) × 2m
Δx = 120° × 2m = 240 meters

Arc Displacement: The object covers an arc displacement of 240 meters.
Example Problem:
An object rotates counterclockwise, starting from an initial angular displacement of 30° to a final angular displacement of 150° in a time interval of 5 seconds. Calculate the angular velocity.

Given:
  • Initial Angular Displacement (θinitial): 30°
  • Final Angular Displacement (θfinal): 150°
  • Time Interval (Δt): 5 seconds

Solution:
ω = θfinal - θinitial / Δt
ω = 150° - 30°/ seconds
ω = 120°/5 seconds = 24°/second

Angular Velocity: The object has an angular velocity of 24°/second.
Convention: Counterclockwise is considered positive, and clockwise is considered negative.

Tangential Acceleration Word Problem

Formula for angular acceleration using tangential acceleration: \(\alpha = \frac{a}{r}\)

Where: "a" is the tangential acceleration (m/s²), "α" is angular acceleration (rad/second²), and "r" is the radius commonly measured as (meters or m)

Note that angular acceleration, tangential acceleration, and centripetal acceleration are not the same thing!

Tangential Acceleration Word Problem

An object is moving in a circular path with a tangential acceleration of 5 m/s² and a radius of 3 meters.

What is the angular acceleration of the object?

Calculate the angular acceleration using the formula: angular acceleration = tangential acceleration / radius.

Solving by Relating it to Angular Velocity

Formula: \(\alpha = \frac{\omega^2}{r}\)

Where: "α" is the angular acceleration (SI unit: rad/s²), "ω" is the angular velocity (SI unit: rad/s), and "r" is the radius (SI unit: m)

Solving by Relating it to Angular Velocity

An object is rotating with an angular velocity of 40 radians per second in a circle with a radius of 4 meters.

What is the angular acceleration of the object?

Calculate the angular acceleration using the formula: angular acceleration = angular velocity² / radius.

Uniform Circular Motion

Formula: \(\alpha = 4 \cdot \pi^2\frac{r}{t^2}\)

Where: "π"(3.14), "r" is the radius (si unit: m ), and "t" is the time (si unit: s)

Uniform Circular Motion

An object is in uniform circular motion with a radius of 6 meters. If the time period is 2 seconds, calculate the angular acceleration.

Use the formula: angular acceleration = 4π²r / t².
